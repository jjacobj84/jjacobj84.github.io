Human trafficking

Through the Lens of Data

What is Human trafficking?

Human trafficking is the acquisition of people by improper means such as force, fraud or deception, with the aim of exploiting them. Trafficking in persons is a serious crime and a grave violation of human rights. According to data published by UNODC (United Nations Office On Drugs and Crime) year 2016, thousands of men, women and children fall into the hands of traffickers, in their own countries and abroad.This visulaziation is based on data obtained from UNODC

No country is immune

Virtually every country in the world is affected by these crimes. Victims are trafficked along a multitude of trafficking flows; within countries, between neighbouring countries or even across different continents. Reasons for trafficking

Based on data reported from 136 countries, trafficking for sexual exploitation and for forced labour are the most prominently detected forms, but trafficking victims can also be exploited in many other ways. Victims are trafficked to be used as beggars, benefit fraud,or for organ removal.
